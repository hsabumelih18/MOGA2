PILLERS

Fisheries and Aquaculture

Improving fisheries by tackling the underlying causes of overfishing and strengthening aquaculture sustainability The overall objective of Pillar 1 is to improve fisheries governance. Activities are divided into two broad categories: 1.Reducing overexploitation of capture fisheries, including by improving transparency, governance, and reducing the impact of harmful fisheries subsidies; 2.Improving aquaculture, including by increasing biosecurity to prevent disease, and supporting new development models in aquaculture

Marine Pollution

Addressing threats posed to ocean health from marine pollution, including litter and plastics, from marine or land-based sources The overall objective of Pillar 2 is the prevention of marine pollution and litter. This requires new policies, changes from consumers and industries, investments, innovations, and private sector interventions. Pillar 2 is prioritizing activities that prevent marine plastics, including: Improving solid waste management to stop leakages; Transitioning to a more circular economy, in which products are designed for reuse and recycling, making waste more valuable; Reducing the upstream production and use of plastics

Oceanic Sectors

Enhancing sustainability of key oceanic sectors such as tourism, maritime transport and offshore renewable energy The overall objective of Pillar 3 is the “blueing” of oceanic sectors, to be achieved by supporting more sustainable development of both: Traditional maritime activities: e.g. shipping and ports, and tourism. More innovative uses: e.g. offshore wind and desalination.
